Martin O'Reilly

Research Software Engineer

Research interests

Reproducible research; Cloud computing; Machine learning; Image processing; Neuroscience;

About me

After a BSc in Physics at Imperial and a year as a travelling salesman in Australia, I worked an analyst and software developer for 5 years, eventually running my own team. While enjoying my time in industry, I wanted to work on problems no one had solved before, so I went back to school - first to a master's in artificial intelligence at Edinburgh and then to a PhD in computational neuroscience at UCL.

Enjoying the research environment but frustrated with the slow pace, superspecialisation and precarious nature of the academic career path, I went back to industry for 3 years as an independent software development consultant. Last November, I discovered research software engineering and the Turing and now marry all my professional interests running the software engineering half of the Turing's Research Engineering team.

Sanna Ojanperä

PhD student

Research interests

international development, digital divide, network science, social data science

About me

Sanna is a first-year doctoral candidate at the Oxford Internet Institute (OII), University of Oxford. She is also a doctoral student at the Alan Turing Institute, where she leads the Data and Inequality interest group together with her supervisor Mark Graham. Before commencing her doctoral studies she worked as the quantitative research lead in an ERC-funded GeoNet project which studies how new economic practices and processes are taking root in Sub-Saharan Africa as a result of changing connectivities.

Before joining the OII, Sanna worked with the Digital Engagement team of the World Bank Governance Global Practice and the Inter-American Development Bank’s Strategic Planning and Development Effectiveness Unit. She earned a Master’s Degree from American University’s School of International Service (Washington, DC) in 2013. At SIS she studied international development, governance, and innovative research methods as an ASLA-Fulbright Fellow.
